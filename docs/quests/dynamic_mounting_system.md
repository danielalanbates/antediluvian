# Dynamic Mounting System: "Creature Mastery"

While the initial questlines in the City of Enoch or the Sethite camps teach the player the *basics* of survival and provide the first taming tools, *Antediluvia* embraces player creativity by allowing an open-ended, sandbox approach to mounts. 

Instead of being locked into a single scripted "Dire-Wolf" mount, players can attempt to tame and ride a vast array of the antediluvian mega-fauna roaming the supercontinent.

## City Hubs: The Source of Knowledge
Cities and major camps are the quest hubs where players learn *how* to interact with the world:
1. **Apprenticeship Quests:** Players receive quests from Beast-Masters in the cities to craft different types of taming gear (e.g., Heavy Iron Chains from Enoch for brutes, or Eden-woven Bridles for swift beasts).
2. **Rumor System:** City NPCs don't just give fetch quests; they give rumors. An injured hunter in the tavern might tell you about a massive, golden-furred cave bear he saw in the northern Hermon foothills. It is up to the player to go out, find it, and figure out how to tame it.

## The Taming Sandbox
To enable player creativity, taming is a dynamic mechanical system rather than a strict quest.

### The Mechanics of Taming
To tame a wild beast, a player must usually:
1. **Analyze:** Discover what the beast eats (e.g., giant boars love toxic mandrake roots).
2. **Weaken/Exhaust:** Fight the beast to lower its stamina or health without killing it.
3. **Subdue:** Use a crafted taming item (like a lasso, chain, or specialized bait) during the critical window.
4. **The Rodeo:** Survive a chaotic minigame or gauntlet where the beast tries to throw the player off before finally submitting.

### Mount Variety & Utility
Because players can choose what they want to ride, mounts are not just "speed boosts." They have distinct physical stats and utilities based on the animal's natural traits:

*   **Dire-Wolves & Felines:** (High Speed, Low Armor). Excellent for crossing the Plains of Nod quickly. They have a short "Pounce" ability to clear small gaps.
*   **Aurochs (Giant Oxen):** (Low Speed, High Capacity). Slower than a wolf, but provides the player with massive extra inventory slots. Ideal for gathering runs in the Wastes.
*   **Cave Bears:** (Medium Speed, High Armor). A combat-oriented mount. Can take several hits from Nephilim giants before the player is forcefully dismounted.
*   **Prehistoric Elephants / Mammoths:** (Multi-Passenger). A massive undertaking to tame, requiring a group of players to subdue. Once tamed, multiple players can ride it at once.
*   **Giant Vultures / Eagles:** (Endgame Gliding). Cannot fly infinitely, but can glide over massive canyons or descend safely from the peaks of Mount Hermon.

## The Mount Stable
Players can stable multiple beasts at the City of Enoch or major nomadic camps, allowing them to swap mounts based on the creative strategies they want to employ for their current adventure.
