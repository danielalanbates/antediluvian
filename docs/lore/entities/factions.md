# The Watchers, Nephilim, and Mortals

This document details the primary entities, factions, and mythological figures that inhabit the antediluvian world, heavily drawing from Second Temple Jewish literature and ancient Near Eastern texts.

## The Watchers (The Grigori)
Originally a class of celestial beings tasked with overseeing humanity, 200 of them defected.
*   **The Oath:** Led by Samyaza, they descended upon Mount Hermon, binding themselves with a curse to take human wives and share forbidden celestial knowledge.
*   **Key Leaders & Their Teachings (The Forbidden Arts):**
    *   **Azazel:** Taught the making of swords, knives, shields, breastplates (metallurgy/weaponry), and the art of cosmetics and jewelry (vanity/seduction). The Warrior class in-game utilizes the corrupted metallurgy of Azazel.
    *   **Samyaza (Shemihazah):** The primary leader. Taught enchantments, sorcery, and root-cuttings (botany/magic). The Mage and Priest classes draw upon (or seek to purify) these arts.
    *   **Baraqiel & Kokabiel:** Taught astrology and the constellations.
    *   **Penemue:** Taught the "bitter and the sweet" and the use of ink and paper (the corruption of writing/contracts).
*   **Fate:** They are ultimately bound in the valleys of the earth until the day of judgment, though their teachings remain to plague humanity.

## The Nephilim (The Giants)
The unnatural offspring of the Watchers and human women.
*   **Nature:** Described in the *Book of Enoch* and the *Book of Giants* as monstrous in both size and appetite. 
*   **The Rampage:** They quickly consumed the food and resources of humanity. When the food ran out, they turned to cannibalism and drinking blood, devastating the earth's ecology (which is reflected in the desolate "Nephilim" Act III zone).
*   **Named Figures (from the Book of Giants):**
    *   **Ohyah and Hahyah:** Brothers who receive prophetic, terrifying dreams about the coming Flood.
    *   **Mahaway:** A giant who acts as an intermediary, seeking out the patriarch Enoch to interpret their dreams.
    *   **Gilgamesh:** The Book of Giants remarkably incorporates this legendary Sumerian king, identifying him as one of the Nephilim, linking biblical lore with Mesopotamian epic tradition.
*   **Post-Flood Legacy:** According to Enochic tradition, when the Nephilim bodies were destroyed in the Flood, their spirits emerged from their corpses to become the "evil spirits" (demons) that roam the earth today.

## Mortal Factions

### The Sethites vs. Cainites
Drawing from early Church Father interpretations (like those of Augustine) and older Jewish traditions:
*   **The Line of Cain:** Builders of the first city (Enoch). They are associated with rapid technological advancement, urbanization, and a departure from the divine order. They were the primary recipients of the Watchers' forbidden teachings.
*   **The Line of Seth:** The lineage from which Noah descends. Traditionally viewed as the "righteous" line that eventually intermingled with the Cainites (the "Sethite" interpretation of Genesis 6), leading to universal corruption.

### The Sumerian Kings (The Antediluvian Rulers)
Based on the *Sumerian King List*, these figures represent the semi-divine, long-lived kings who ruled the pre-flood cities.
*   **Alulim & Alalngar:** The earliest kings, ruling for tens of thousands of years in Eridu.
*   **Enmeduranki:** The seventh antediluvian king of Sippar, associated with the sun god and divine divination. He heavily parallels the biblical Enoch (the seventh from Adam), reflecting a shared ancient tradition of a highly exalted seventh patriarch who receives heavenly secrets.
