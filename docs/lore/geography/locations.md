# Antediluvian Geography: The Pre-Flood World

The geography of Antediluvia is fundamentally based on the concept that the antediluvian world was a distinct, original landmass that was completely destroyed and reconfigured by the Great Flood. Thus, while some names echo into the post-flood world (like the Tigris/Euphrates in Genesis, or Uruk/Eridu in Sumerian tradition), the physical landscapes of the game represent a lost world.

## The Five Acts (Zones)

The game's progression is a physical and spiritual descent, mirroring the timeline of the Watchers' corruption.

### 1. Act I: Eden (The Remnant)
*   **Speculated Time/Era:** The early centuries after the expulsion from the Garden, before the Watchers' descent.
*   **Lore:** Eden is not the Garden itself (which is barred by cherubim), but the surrounding lands where early humanity first settled. It is a place of natural beauty, soft green-gold light, and relative innocence.
*   **Key Features:** Lush vegetation, simple agrarian settlements, and peaceful wildlife. The corruption here is subtle, primarily natural predators and early human strife (echoing the legacy of Cain).

### 2. Act II: Hermon (The Descent)
*   **Speculated Time/Era:** The days of Jared (when the Watchers descended).
*   **Lore:** Mount Hermon is the true geographical epicenter of the game's conflict. It is here that the 200 Watchers, led by Samyaza, made their mutual oath of imprecation to rebel against heaven and take human wives.
*   **Key Features:** High altitude, cool blue light, rocky terrain. This zone features the first encounters with early Watcher-cults and the initial signs of forbidden celestial knowledge being practiced by humanity. 
*   **Theological Link:** In later traditions, the base of Hermon (Caesarea Philippi) is linked to the "Gates of Hades," making this mountain a literal gateway for spiritual incursion.

### 3. Act III: Nephilim (The Proliferation)
*   **Speculated Time/Era:** The era of rapid Nephilim expansion and resource depletion.
*   **Lore:** The offspring of the Watchers and women, the Nephilim, have grown into a civilization of giants. They are insatiable, consuming the earth's resources and turning to violence and cannibalism when food runs out.
*   **Key Features:** A dusty, red, chaotic wasteland. The land is scarred by immense resource extraction (mining for Watcher metallurgy). Enemy types shift heavily toward giant variants and corrupted flora/fauna.

### 4. Act IV: Enoch (The Corrupted City)
*   **Speculated Time/Era:** The height of antediluvian civilization, shortly before the Flood.
*   **Lore:** Named after the first city built by Cain (Genesis 4:17), Enoch represents the peak of human technological advancement fueled by the forbidden arts of the Watchers. Here, Azazel's teachings (metallurgy, weapons, armor) and Samyaza's (sorcery, root-cuttings) are mastered by corrupted human warlords.
*   **Key Features:** A smoggy, gray, oppressive urban environment. It features advanced ancient architecture, intense military presence, and powerful sorcerers. (This contrasts with the patriarch Enoch, who warned the giants of their doom).

### 5. Act V: Flood (The Judgment)
*   **Speculated Time/Era:** The final days/hours before the Deluge.
*   **Lore:** The corruption of the Watchers and Nephilim has reached its breaking point, and divine judgment is initiated. 
*   **Key Features:** Storm-dark skies, constant heavy rain, rising waters, and a sense of apocalyptic urgency. The enemies here are desperate, fighting for survival as the deep begins to break open.

## Theoretical Influences
*   **Book of Enoch:** Mount Hermon as the site of the Watchers' oath.
*   **Genesis:** The City of Enoch (built by Cain) as the archetype of human civilization devoid of God.
*   **Sumerian King List:** The idea of ancient, powerful, pre-flood city-states (like Eridu or Bad-tibira) that possessed semi-divine kingship.
