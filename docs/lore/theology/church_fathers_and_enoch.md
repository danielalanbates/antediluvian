# Theological and Mythological Frameworks

The world of Antediluvia is built upon ancient interpretations of the early chapters of Genesis, specifically how the Second Temple Jewish community and the early Christian Church understood the primeval history of the world.

## The Origin of Evil

A central theological problem addressed by antediluvian literature (specifically 1 Enoch and Jubilees) is the origin and persistence of evil.

*   **The Adamic Fall vs. The Angelic Fall:** While modern Western theology primarily focuses on the Fall of Adam and Eve as the sole origin of sin, Second Temple Judaism placed equal, if not greater, emphasis on the sin of the Watchers (Genesis 6:1-4).
*   **The Corruption of Knowledge:** Evil was not merely a moral failing of humanity; it was introduced via illicit, celestial technology. The Watchers taught humans things they were not meant to know (metallurgy, weaponry, sorcery), weaponizing human civilization.
*   **The Origin of Demons:** The Book of Enoch provides the definitive ancient explanation for demons. They are not fallen angels themselves, but the disembodied spirits of the Nephilim. When the giants died (or were killed in the Flood), their hybrid nature meant their spirits could neither ascend to heaven nor rest in the earth, leaving them to wander as "evil spirits" that afflict humanity to this day.

## Early Church Fathers and the "Sons of God"

The interpretation of the "sons of God" who fathered the Nephilim underwent a major shift in early Christian history.

### 1. The Angelic Interpretation (1st - 3rd Century)
*   **Proponents:** Justin Martyr, Irenaeus, Clement of Alexandria, Tertullian, Athenagoras.
*   **The View:** Heavily influenced by the Book of Enoch, these early fathers believed the "sons of God" were literal celestial beings (Watchers) who abandoned their post to mate with human women. 
*   **Significance:** This view provided a robust framework for understanding spiritual warfare, the demonic realm, and why the ancient world was so thoroughly corrupted that God had to destroy it.

### 2. The Sethite Interpretation (4th Century Onward)
*   **Proponents:** Augustine of Hippo, Julius Africanus.
*   **The View:** As the Church distanced itself from apocryphal texts like Enoch, and grappled with the metaphysical impossibility of spirits procreating with flesh, a new view emerged. The "sons of God" were reinterpreted as the righteous, human descendants of Seth. The "daughters of men" were the unrighteous descendants of Cain. The Nephilim were simply the result of this unholy intermingling of the faithful and unfaithful lines.
*   **Significance:** This became the dominant view in Western Christianity for over a millennium, framing the antediluvian conflict as a moral and religious schism rather than a cosmic, supernatural invasion.

*Note: Antediluvia (the game) firmly adopts the older, **Angelic Interpretation** (the Enochic framework), as it provides the necessary mythological, supernatural, and fantastical elements required for a dark fantasy setting.*

## The Role of the Flood

In the Enochic and Jubilees traditions, the Flood is not merely a punishment for human sinfulness (as commonly taught today). It is a necessary, cosmic cleansing.
*   **Eradicating the Nephilim:** The primary purpose of the Deluge was to wipe out the giant offspring, whose violence and consumption were destroying the planet.
*   **Washing away the "Blood":** The earth itself is depicted as crying out to heaven because of the bloodshed caused by the Watchers' weapons and the giants' cannibalism. The Flood is a baptism of the earth, washing away the physical corruption before humanity could start anew.
