# Antediluvian Lore: Foundational Texts and Influences

This document compiles the core research and mythological underpinnings that inform the setting, acts, and narrative of the world of Antediluvia. The game's world is a fantasy interpretation of the "antediluvian" (pre-flood) era, drawing heavily from apocryphal texts, ancient Near Eastern mythology, and biblical tradition.

## Primary Sources & Texts

### 1. The Book of Enoch (1 Enoch)
The Book of Enoch is the most significant source of inspiration for the game's lore, particularly the "Book of the Watchers" (the first section).
*   **The Watchers (Grigori):** A class of angels dispatched to earth to watch over humanity. Instead, 200 of them defected, lusted after human women, and bound themselves with a mutual oath on the summit of Mount Hermon to follow through with their rebellion.
*   **The Nephilim:** The unnatural offspring of the Watchers and human women. Described as giant, violent beings with insatiable appetites who consumed the earth's resources and eventually turned on humanity and each other.
*   **Forbidden Arts:** The Watchers taught humanity forbidden celestial knowledge, corrupting the early world. 
    *   *Azazel* taught metallurgy (making swords, knives, shields, breastplates) and cosmetics/jewelry.
    *   *Samyaza* (Shemihazah) taught root cuttings and sorcery/spells.
    *   *Baraqiel* taught astrology.
    *   *Kokabiel* taught the signs of the stars.
*   **Theological Role:** Provides an explanation for the origin of persistent evil and demonic spirits (which are said to be the disembodied spirits of the dead Nephilim).

### 2. The Book of Jubilees
Often called the "Lesser Genesis," this text was highly influential in the Second Temple period (found among the Dead Sea Scrolls).
*   **Chronology:** It attempts to provide a strict, rigid chronology of the antediluvian patriarchs, divided into "jubilees" (periods of 49 years).
*   **Law and Order:** It emphasizes that the angels initially descended to instruct humanity in order and righteousness, making their eventual fall a tragic perversion of their original divine mandate.

### 3. The Sumerian King List
A vital comparative text from ancient Mesopotamia that lists rulers and incredibly long reign lengths.
*   **Parallels:** Like Genesis, it divides history into "before the flood" and "after the flood."
*   **Longevity:** Antediluvian kings in the Sumerian list reigned for tens of thousands of years (e.g., Alulim for 28,800 years), reflecting a shared ancient Near Eastern motif of extreme longevity or symbolic astronomical numbering in the primeval past. Genesis 5 patriarchs (like Methuselah living 969 years) reflect a similar, though less mathematically extreme, tradition.
*   **The Seventh Figure:** In both traditions, the seventh figure in the lineage is uniquely connected to the divine realm. In the Sumerian King List, it is Enmeduranki, associated with divine divination. In Genesis, it is Enoch, who "walked with God" and was taken alive.

## The Acts of Antediluvia

The game is structured into five distinct Acts (zones), which map to this apocryphal geography and narrative progression:

1.  **Eden:** The starting zone. A soft, green-gold remnant of the original paradise, representing the early dawn of humanity before the full corruption of the Watchers took hold.
2.  **Hermon:** A cool, mountainous, blue-tinged zone. This is the literal epicenter of the rebellion—the peak where the 200 Watchers descended, made their pact, and first intermingled with humanity.
3.  **Nephilim:** A dusty, red, chaotic zone. This represents the rampant spread of the giant offspring. It is a land of violence, resource depletion, and war, where the corrupted teachings of the Watchers (weaponry, sorcery) are in full effect.
4.  **Enoch:** A smoggy, gray city zone. Named for the patriarch who warned the Watchers of their doom. It represents advanced but corrupted human civilization—the peak of the forbidden arts and metallurgy taught by Azazel.
5.  **Flood:** The final zone. Storm-dark, rainy, and apocalyptic. It represents the impending divine judgment sent to cleanse the earth of the Watchers' corruption and the violence of the Nephilim.

## Implementation Notes for the Game
*   **Classes/Professions:** The teachings of the Watchers directly inspire the game's systems. Warriors use the metallurgy of Azazel; Mages/Priests use the sorcery and root-cuttings of Samyaza.
*   **Enemies:** The "Nephilim" act and beyond should feature giant-type enemies (like the `Skeleton_Warrior_alpha` or future models) representing the offspring of the Watchers.
*   **Atmosphere:** The lighting moods defined in `atmosphere.rs` perfectly track this narrative descent from the soft light of Eden to the apocalyptic storm of the Flood.
